Product Detail

Liners:
These flat, outer layers made from heavy paperboard form the box's face and back, providing rigidity and structural support. Imagine them as sturdy bookends holding the story together. .

Fluting:
The wavy, corrugated middle layer, made from thinner paperboard, acts as the shock absorber. Its peaks and valleys distribute pressure and cushion contents, like a spring mattress cradling your body.

Adhesive Bond:
This invisible force binding the liners and fluting is the box's backbone. Without it, the components would crumble. Think of it as the thread weaving the story, ensuring a unified box.

Score Lines:
These faint creases on the liners guide easy assembly, allowing the flat sheet to transform into a box with simple folds. Imagine them as pre-written chapter titles, guiding the box's transformation.

Customizations:
Die-cut handles for carrying, inserts for specific items, and printed graphics further extend the box's versatility, tailoring it to its contents. Think of them as plot twists, adding unique dimensions to the box's purpose
